Two-Step Verification adds a password on top of your SMS or OTP login code. It protects your account if someone manages to get your login code.

## Steps in Telegram

### 1. Open `Settings`

Start in Telegram and open the `Settings` screen.

![Telegram settings step](/assets/articles/2Factor/step-1-settings.jpg)

### 2. Choose `Privacy and Security`

Open `Privacy and Security` to reach the account protection options.

![Privacy and Security step](/assets/articles/2Factor/step-2-privacy&security.jpg)

### 3. Set a password for Two-Step Verification

Telegram will ask you to create a new password. Use one that is different from your other PINs or account passwords.

![Set password step](/assets/articles/2Factor/step-3-put-password.jpg)

## Summary

1. Open Telegram and go to `Settings`
2. Choose `Privacy and Security`
3. Tap `Two-Step Verification`
4. Create a password that is different from your other PINs or passwords
5. Add a recovery email if you can

## After you enable it

Do not share this password with anyone. If a message claims it needs that password, treat it as a scam by default.
